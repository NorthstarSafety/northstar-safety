# Northstar Objections

## "We already have folders and spreadsheets for this."

Response:

That is exactly the gap Northstar is meant to close. The issue is not whether the documents exist. The issue is whether one person can see, by SKU, what is missing, what signals matter, and what actions were taken without stitching it together manually.

## "We do not have recalls very often."

Response:

The recurring value is not just recalls. It is keeping evidence and review history attached to the live catalog every month, so when a signal or retailer question does show up, the team is not starting from zero.

## "The recall issue is already completed."

Response:

That is fair, and it usually means the problem is not urgent enough for a fire-drill sale. The relevant question is whether the team wants a cleaner monthly operating system before the next retailer question, supplier issue, or official alert appears.

## "We already use a lab or consultant."

Response:

Northstar does not replace labs or legal counsel. It gives the internal team one operating layer for the work between Shopify, the evidence packet, and the official signal review.

## "We are not ready for a big rollout."

Response:

That is why the pilot is one store, one owner, and the first 10 priority SKUs. The scope is intentionally narrow.

## "We do not want a services project."

Response:

Northstar is scoped as software-led onboarding, not a custom compliance engagement. The founder helps set up the first workflow, then the merchant uses the same workspace for the monthly process.

## "How much internal effort will this take?"

Response:

The merchant needs one Shopify connection, one internal owner, and the first evidence packet. That is the minimum needed to get to value without dragging the process out.

## "Is this legal advice?"

Response:

No. Northstar is an internal operating system for evidence, review, and action logging. Legal interpretation and filings stay outside the product.

## "Why should we pay before every integration is perfect?"

Response:

Because the pilot already solves the live workflow that hurts first: catalog visibility, missing evidence, recall review, and action trail. The goal of the pilot is to prove that value on your real catalog before anything broader is built.

## "What if the match is a false positive?"

Response:

That is part of the workflow. Northstar expects false positives, gives the operator a clean review step, and keeps written dismissal reasons so the history gets more useful over time.

## "Can this handle Canada too?"

Response:

The workflow and jurisdiction model already support US plus Canada. The next product gap is deeper live Canada-source coverage, which is already on the post-pilot backlog.

## "We are a small brand and the budget is tight."

Response:

That is why the founding pilot is a simple `$300` one-time payment instead of a bigger onboarding project. It gives your team 30 days to prove the workflow on live SKUs before any longer-term decision is even discussed.
