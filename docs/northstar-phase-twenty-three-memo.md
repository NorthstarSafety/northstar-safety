# Northstar Phase 23 Memo

Date: April 8, 2026

## What changed

Phase 23 pivoted Northstar away from broad low-yield outreach and into three higher-signal channels:

1. partner-led pipeline
2. trigger-based brand outreach
3. stronger direct contact routes for top accounts

The work this phase emphasized actual contact motion over more list-building.

## What ran

### Partner-led pipeline

Live partner-first outreach sent:

- QIMA via live contact form
- Best Practice Quality via live contact form

Why these were chosen first:

- both are already adjacent to compliance, testing, or product-quality pain
- both had reachable live contact surfaces
- both can plausibly refer, forward, or introduce Northstar into a child-product workflow already in motion

### Trigger-based brand outreach

A fresh trigger-led subset was defined in:

- [northstar_phase23_trigger_leads.csv](C:/Users/onkha/OneDrive/Documents/New project/output/spreadsheet/northstar_phase23_trigger_leads.csv)

The strongest trigger this phase was category pressure around bassinets, cribs, strollers, and other juvenile-product lines where evidence retrieval and alert review matter more when official standards or category recalls are visible.

### Higher-signal direct contact

Live higher-signal routes used:

- Cradlewise via Intercom on the support center
- Stokke via live site chatbot

Cradlewise received a short founder-led message tied to current bassinet category pressure.

Stokke produced the best concrete signal of the phase:

- the chatbot responded with a routing instruction to `partnerships@stokke.com`

That is not a booked demo yet, but it is a real team path that did not exist under the previous generic contact-form approach.

## Channel readout

Strongest channel this phase:

1. higher-signal live chat / messenger surfaces

Why:

- they put the message in front of an active support or routing system immediately
- they returned either a live thread or a concrete team handoff
- they felt less buried than generic website forms

Second strongest:

2. partner-first forms

Why:

- the partner value proposition is clearer than cold brand outreach
- a forwarded intro or shared client pilot is more believable than asking a cold merchant to care from scratch

Still blocked:

- direct founder-email follow-through in-browser
  - the Gmail browser session is still at sign-in, so routed email follow-ups cannot yet be executed from the Workspace inbox inside this browser session

## What should be pushed hardest next

1. Partner-first outreach to more labs, consultants, and agencies with live forms
2. Trigger-led high-signal chats on top juvenile-product accounts, especially where support chat is reachable
3. Immediate founder-domain follow-through to routed addresses like `partnerships@stokke.com` once the Workspace inbox is reopened in-browser

## Bottom line

Phase 23 created a better conversation path than the old generic outreach loop. The most important proof is that higher-signal routes are now returning useful routing information instead of silence. Northstar should keep leaning into partner-first outreach and trigger-led direct contact, not back into broad low-context form volume.
