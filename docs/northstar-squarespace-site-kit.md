### Solution Section

Section eyebrow:

- What Northstar does

Headline:

Three columns:

- Sync the catalog
  Copy: Pull products and variants from Shopify so every live SKU has a home in the system.

- Track product evidence
  Copy: Keep product documents and missing items visible at the SKU level so the team knows what is ready and what still needs attention.

- Review alerts and actions
  Copy: Review official safety signals, record false positives, open investigations, and keep a clear internal trail.

### How It Works Section

Section eyebrow:

- How it works

Headline:

- A simple workflow from catalog to action.

Steps:

- 1. Connect Shopify
  Copy: Start from the real store catalog, not a spreadsheet export.

- 2. Organize evidence
  Copy: Keep product documents and missing items attached to the right SKU.

- 3. Review official signals
  Copy: See likely overlaps and work through them with context.

- 4. Track investigations
  Copy: Record owners, notes, decisions, and follow-up actions in one place.

- FAQ

Headline:

- Common questions

### Section: What the Product Covers

Headline:

- The core workflow Northstar owns

Intro:

- Northstar is not trying to do everything. It is designed to own one recurring workflow well.

Four feature blocks:

- Shopify catalog sync
  Copy: Bring products and variants into Northstar from the live store so the workspace reflects what the merchant is actually selling.

- Product evidence tracking
  Copy: Keep product-level evidence in one place and make missing items visible instead of discovering gaps late.

- Official signal review
  Copy: Pull in official recall and safety signals, then review likely overlaps with practical context.

- Investigation and action trail
  Copy: Track what was reviewed, who owns it, what decision was made, and what happened next.

### Section: Why this is better than the usual stack

Headline:

- Better than a patchwork process

Comparison rows:

- Shared drives
  Copy: Good for storage, not for knowing what is missing by SKU.

- Spreadsheets
  Copy: Good for lists, not for connected evidence, alerts, and actions.

- Inbox review
  Copy: Good for one message, not for a repeatable operating trail.

- Northstar
  Copy: Built to keep the product record, the evidence record, the alert review, and the action record tied together.

### Section: A normal monthly use case

Headline:

- What using Northstar looks like month to month

Body:

- A team syncs the live catalog, sees which priority SKUs have missing product evidence, refreshes official safety signals, reviews likely overlaps, dismisses false positives with written reasoning, and opens a case when a product needs investigation. The result is a cleaner operating rhythm and a reusable internal record.

### Section: Data and trust

Headline:

- Built to be practical and trustworthy

Points:

- Starts from the real Shopify catalog
- Focuses on official safety and recall workflows
- Keeps internal notes and decisions attached to the right product
- Uses a narrow scope instead of asking for unnecessary merchant data

### Section: FAQ

Questions and answers:

- Is this a compliance platform for every regulation?
  Answer: No. Northstar is intentionally focused on the recurring operational work between product evidence, signal review, and investigation tracking.

- Can Northstar be used before a recall happens?
  Answer: Yes. The evidence workflow is useful even when no urgent event exists.

- Who usually owns this inside a brand?
  Answer: Usually a founder, operations lead, quality lead, or product owner.

Headline:

- See the workflow on a real catalog

## 12. Suggested Visual Direction for Squarespace

Brand mood:

- calm
- minimal
- modern
- trustworthy
- corporate without feeling cold

Color direction:

- deep navy
- soft stone
- warm white
- muted slate
- restrained safety green as an accent

Suggested palette:

- Primary: `#10243E`
- Secondary text: `#4A5868`
- Background: `#F6F4EF`
- Surface: `#FFFFFF`
- Accent: `#4D7C68`
- Divider: `#D8D4CC`

Typography direction:

- clean serif for headlines
- neutral sans-serif for body

Example pairings available in Squarespace-style systems:

- Headline: a modern serif with authority
- Body: a clean grotesk or humanist sans

Layout direction:

- generous spacing
- strong section rhythm
- quiet backgrounds
- simple product illustration or diagram blocks instead of flashy mockups
- clear forms and restrained CTA buttons

Image direction:

- product screenshots framed cleanly
- workflow diagrams
- detail crops of calm, minimal UI
- avoid generic stock-team photography if possible
